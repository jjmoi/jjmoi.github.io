me to Nick2 days ago

The startup scene is growing fast but VCs are still hesitating. Telecom and banking companies are the leading players in funding startups. Each of the top ones have their own incubators. A handful of the successful startups also get money from Singapore or Japan or partnered with them. But very small percentage of them care much about design. Most of them are very tech/dev minded and admire anything Silicon Valley.

The understanding of design is better among the designers but sadly still a long way to go for clients. Last month, an HCI Professor from Zurich University got funding to help kickstarting a formal HCI group in Thailand.

There're 2 major communities that I'm aware of: UX in Thailand and BKKWeb. They are kinda active and throw a meetup every month or so but most are in Thai. A handful of members here are fluent in English. There is also a group for expats but not as active, and many posts are just spam/self-promotion. But they do have good meetups.

You said you'll be there for a couple years. Is it a long-term contract job or are you freelancing? I can also point you to good companies that value design if you'll be looking for a job.
