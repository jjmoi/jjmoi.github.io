Based in the deep woods of the Pacific Northwest, bringing design sense to deep tech, working with breakthrough scientific companies transforming billions of lives.

Designer, educator, investor, and advisor to startups reinventing climate tech, in-space transportation, quantum computing, robotics, nanomaterials, AI, and life science. Prior, designed digital services for the people as an innovation specialist at 18F and built the world's leading open data platforms as the founding designer at Tachyus and OpenGov. In a past life, art historian, graffiti artist, capoeirista, and turtle. Spirit animal, mermaid. Allergic to cats.

My work has been to space, recognized by the Smithsonian and the World Economic Forum, and appeared in a number of publications, including NYT, CNN, BBC, WSJ, WaPo, PopSci, and a few other acronyms. Currently guiding aspiring designers as they navigate through education as a lecturer at Pratt Institute as well as serving as a guest critic and mentor at University of Washington HCDE, Carnegie Mellon University HCII, and Oregon State University School of Design Arts. Also co-founded The Intersection, a design apprenticeship program fostering the next generation of creative entrepreneurs committed to making the world a more inclusive and beautiful place.
