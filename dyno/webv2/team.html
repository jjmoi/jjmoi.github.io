Eric Kelsic, PhD

Cofounder and CEO

Prior to founding Dyno, Eric led a team to develop the technology underlying Dyno’s machine-guided protein engineering platform as a Research Scientist in George Church’s lab at the Wyss Institute of Harvard Medical School. He holds a PhD in Systems Biology from Harvard University and a BS in Physics from Caltech. During his PhD he studied higher order interactions among soil microbes and discovered a new mechanism for the maintenance of ecological diversity, and developed MAGE-Seq, a method combining multiplexed genome-engineering with massively parallel phenotyping. He has published in top journals including Science and Nature. As an undergrad he led one of the first studies of network structure on facebook social networks Puzzle boxes.

Shimyn Slomovic, PhD

Cofounder and Head of Biology

Shimyn is a Molecular and Synthetic Biologist with broad expertise in biological research and application. He earned his BA and PhD in Biology at the Technion, Israel Institute of Technology, where his research on RNA metabolism led to the discovery of a degradation pathway in human mitochondria, for which he received the Fine, Gutwirt, and Wolf awards. As a post doc fellow at the Synthetic biology lab of James Collins at MIT, Shimyn was the recipient of a Ragon Institute of MGH, MIT, and Harvard fellowship, and engineered a DNA sense and respond system in human cells that enabled pathogen detection. He also helped pioneer a platform for portable, on-demand biomolecular manufacturing of antimicrobial peptides and vaccines for field clinics and low-resource settings. As a Research Scientist at the Wyss Institute of Harvard Medical School, Shimyn devised a method for at-home HIV testing using instant film as a readout. When not working with the amazing Dyno team, Shimyn enjoys nature walks and spending time with his family.

Sam Sinai, Phd

Cofounder and Lead ML Scientist

Sam is a computational biologist with expertise in evolution and machine learning. He received his PhD under George Church and Martin Nowak at Harvard, where he used mathematics and machine learning to develop models of biological systems. In particular, he developed the underpinnings of Dyno’s machine learning strategy in collaboration with Eric, and has co-authored multiple papers on the subject. He previously obtained his B.S. and M.Eng. in computer science at MIT with a focus on A.I., and had a brief stint in medical school. Sam enjoys techno, soccer, and philosophy.

Adrian Veres

Scientific Founder and Advisor

Adrian is a biologist most satisfied by combining experimental and computational approaches to study complex systems. He obtained his PhD in Systems Biology from Harvard University, working with Prof. Douglas Melton on differentiating pancreatic beta cells in vitro to create cell-based therapies for diabetes. He collaborated closely with Semma Therapeutics on applying single-cell RNA sequencing to map in vitro beta cell differentiation, and pooled genetic screening to control this process. Adrian is a Harvard University Presidential Scholar and previously received a Harvard Stem Cell Institute Medical Scientist Training Fellowship. He graduated summa cum laude with an A.B. in Physics and Chemistry from Harvard College, during, during which he studied the evolutionary dynamics of antibiotic resistance in microbes. He is an MD candidate in the Harvard-MIT Health Sciences and Technology program. Adrian loves to cook, travel and hike and always enjoys a good new board game.
