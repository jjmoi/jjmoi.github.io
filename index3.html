Based in the deep woods of the Pacific Northwest, bringing design sense to deep tech, working with breakthrough scientific startups transforming billions of lives at the intersection of 126th Ave and Broadway.

Design Partner at Prime Movers Lab, a venture capital fund with over $1b under management, reinventing climate tech, in-space transportation, quantum computing, robotics, nanomaterials, AI, and longevity. Before that, designing digital services for the people as Innovation Specialist at 18F and building the world's leading open data platforms as founding designer at Tachyus and OpenGov. In a past life, art historian, graffiti artist, capoeirista, and turtle. Spirit animal, mermaid. Allergic to cats.

My work has been to space, recognized by the Smithsonian and the World Economic Forum, and featured in NYT, CNN, BBC, WSJ, WaPo, PopSci, and a couple other media acronyms. Maybe one day, NPR. Continuing to mentor aspiring designers as they navigate through their education and careers. Taught at Pratt Institute and advised students at University of Washington and Carnegie Mellon University. Also co-founded Intersection, a design apprenticeship platform fostering the next generation of designer entrepreneurs committed to making the world a more inclusive place.
